# Forensic Financial Ratio Methodology
**Author:** Henry C. Dibie
**Focus:** Construction Sector Liquidity & Solvency Analysis

This document outlines the quantitative logic used to evaluate corporate creditworthiness. In the construction industry, standard ratios must be viewed through the lens of project-based cash flows and "lumpy" revenue recognition.

## 1. Liquidity & Working Capital Analysis
Liquidity is the primary "anchor" for credit ratings. For this framework, we analyze the gap between theoretical liquidity and "cash-on-hand" reality.

* **Current Ratio (Formula: Current Assets / Current Liabilities)**
    * **Threshold:** > 1.25 is target.
    * **Strategic Interpretation:** A drop from 1.66 to 1.14 (as seen in the sample) signals that capital is being aggressively deployed into new projects. While it looks "risky" to a bureau, it represents **Growth Capacity**.
* **The "Safety Buffer" (Working Capital)**
    * **Logic:** We measure the absolute SGD value of Net Working Capital to determine how many months of "burn rate" (Opex) the company can sustain if a Tier-1 developer delays a milestone payment.

## 2. Solvency & Leverage (The Debt-to-Equity Bridge)
Bureaus penalize high leverage. My methodology focuses on **Debt Quality.**

* **Debt-to-Equity Ratio (Formula: Total Liabilities / Shareholders’ Equity)**
    * **Logic:** We differentiate between "Good Debt" (Asset-backed financing for heavy machinery) and "Bad Debt" (Unsecured credit lines used for payroll).
* **Interest Coverage Ratio (Formula: EBIT / Interest Expense)**
    * **Strategic Use:** We use this to prove to lenders that despite a CA3 rating, the company generates **4x more profit** than required to service its current debt interest.

## 3. Profitability & Operational Efficiency
Revenue growth without margin expansion is a "Growth Trap."

* **Net Profit Margin (Formula: Net Income / Total Revenue)**
    * **Analysis:** Moving from 2.5% to 4%+ proves that the company has achieved **Economies of Scale**. This is the primary argument for a credit rating upgrade.
* **Asset Turnover Ratio (Formula: Revenue / Total Assets)**
    * **Logic:** This measures how hard the company is "working" its equipment. In construction, high turnover with rising margins indicates elite project management.

## 4. The "Mismatched Term" Identification (DSO vs. DPO)
This is the "secret sauce" of the audit.
* **Days Sales Outstanding (DSO):** Time to collect cash from clients.
* **Days Payable Outstanding (DPO):** Time taken to pay suppliers.
* **The Delta:** If DSO is 90 days and DPO is 30 days, the company is "funding" its clients' projects with its own cash. My methodology identifies this gap and recommends shifting DPO to 60 days to align with the industry cycle.

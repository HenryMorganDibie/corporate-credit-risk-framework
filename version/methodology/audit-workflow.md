# Corporate Credit Audit & Governance Workflow

**Framework:** The 6-Month Reciprocal Review Cycle
**Author:** Henry C. Dibie

This document details the operational workflow used to bridge the gap between financial analysis and regulatory credit reporting. It ensures that credit improvement is not a "one-time fix" but a sustainable governance practice.

## Phase 1: The Baseline Forensic Audit (Month 0)

The objective is to establish a "source of truth" by reconciling external bureau data with internal financial records.

1. **Bureau Data Decomposition:**
   - **Public Record Scrubbing:** Identifying legal suits, bankruptcy filings, or mortgages. For the Singapore sector, this involves checking the "Writs of Summons" and "Winding Up" status.
   - **Payment Profile Analysis:** Calculating the **Payment Score (1–100)** based on trade credit data. We categorise delays into *Administrative* (processing lags) vs. *Systemic* (cash flow failure).

2. **Financial Reality Check:**
   - Cross-referencing the SCCB report's "Revenue/Profit" figures against the company's audited Financial Statements.
   - Identifying discrepancies in "Paid-up Capital" that may not have been updated in the bureau's database.

## Phase 2: Actionable Strategy Mapping

We translate the findings into a prioritised **11-Point Improvement Plan**, categorised by impact:

- **CP (Credit Profile):** High-impact, low-effort fixes (e.g., updating legal status, correcting company addresses, confirming litigation discontinuance records).
- **FM (Financial Management):** Medium-term balance sheet adjustments (e.g., restructuring short-term liabilities into longer-duration facilities, improving the Current Ratio toward the ≥1.50x target).
- **PM (Payment Management):** Operational changes (e.g., renegotiating supplier terms to reduce the payment period below 60 days and directly improve the SCCB Payment Score).

## Phase 3: The Implementation Period (Months 1–5)

During this phase, the consultant acts as a **Risk Controller**, monitoring the execution of the 11-point plan:

- **Monthly Cash Flow Reviews:** Ensuring the company maintains the recommended liquidity buffer, structured around project completion milestones rather than a standard rolling forecast.
- **Evidence Gathering:** Collecting receipts, settlement letters, and updated bank statements to serve as "Proof of Improvement" for the next bureau submission.

## Phase 4: The 6-Month Progress Audit (The Verification)

This is the final step where we quantify the success of the framework.

1. **Requirement of "Fresh" Data:** Ordering a new Comprehensive Report from the bureau to measure the delta (change) in the Credit Rating and Payment Score.
2. **Ratio Recalculation:** We re-run the Liquidity, Solvency, and Profitability ratios using the most recent 6 months of management accounts.
3. **The Verdict:**
   - **Green (Implemented):** Recommendation fully integrated and reflected in bureau data.
   - **Amber (In Progress):** Policy adopted but not yet reflected in bureau scoring.
   - **Red (Outstanding):** Risk still present — escalated in the next action plan.

## Phase 5: Reciprocal Bureau Submission

The final step is the preparation of a **Forensic Rebuttal Package** for the credit reporting agency. This package contains:

- The **Progress Audit Report** as an exhibit of corporate governance.
- Direct evidence of **Litigation Discontinuance** (certified court documents for all settled suits).
- A **24-month Trend Analysis** showing the V-shaped revenue and profit recovery.
- A request for a **Manual Rating Override** based on updated Financial Ratio parity — citing the internal contradiction between improved credit limits and a static rating.

---

*This workflow ensures that the company does not just "ask" for a better credit score, but demonstrates a verifiable increase in creditworthiness through disciplined financial governance.*

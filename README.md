# Corporate Credit Risk Analysis & Strategic Improvement Framework

**Sector focus: Construction & Engineering (Singapore)**

## 📌 Executive Overview

This repository showcases a professional framework for converting raw commercial credit bureau data into a high-level corporate financial strategy. The project centres on a **V-shaped recovery case study** where a construction firm achieved a **+145% revenue increase** (SGD 57.6M → SGD 141.1M) and a **+284% net profit surge** (SGD 1.48M → SGD 5.69M), yet remained stagnant at an "Average Risk" (CA3) credit rating — despite its recommended credit limit increasing by **120%**.

The objective of this framework is to provide the analytical evidence required for credit bureau disputes and to implement internal governance controls that align a company's operational reality with its credit score.

---

## 🛠 Strategic Methodology (The "Henry C. Dibie" Framework)

I employ a three-tier approach to corporate credit risk management:

### 1. The Paradox Analysis (Quantitative)

Traditional credit scoring models often lag behind rapid operational growth. I identify **"Credit Anchors"** — specific metrics that are preventing a rating upgrade despite strong profitability.

- **Metric Reconciliation:** Analysing why the *Recommended Credit Limit* increased by 120% while the *Credit Rating* remained "Held." A 120% limit increase without a rating upgrade is an internal contradiction — and the basis of a formal rating parity challenge.
- **Liquidity Stress Testing:** Evaluating the structural impact of rapid contract scaling on working capital. In this case study, current liabilities surged 164% (SGD 17.5M → SGD 46.2M) as the Company funded growth through short-term trade credit — compressing the current ratio from 1.67x to 1.28x.
- **Key anchor metrics tracked:** Current Ratio (target ≥ 1.50x), Debt-to-Equity (target < 200%), Interest Coverage (160.82x — exceptional), SCCB Payment Score (59/100 → target 70+/100).

### 2. Forensic Dispute Preparation (Qualitative)

Not all "Slow Payments" are created equal. I analyse payment history to differentiate between:

- **Administrative Friction:** Small-balance delays that trigger automated score drops but carry no financial distress signal.
- **Industry Standards:** Identifying **contra-settlement models** common in construction that skew average payment days (DSO/DPO). In this engagement, the Company collects receivables in 47 days but pays suppliers in 81 days — a deliberate working capital float, not a cash flow failure.
- **Litigation Scrubbing:** Verifying the status of withdrawn or discontinued legal claims (all suits in this case were workplace/vehicular — not financial disputes) to clear "Public Record" red flags.

### 3. The 6-Month Progress Audit (Governance)

A credit score is a lagging indicator. To ensure long-term improvement, I implement an **11-point accountability framework** categorised into:

- **CP (Credit Profile):** Bureau data correction and litigation record updates.
- **FM (Financial Management):** Capital structure and liability optimisation (e.g., restructuring current trade payables into medium-term facilities).
- **PM (Payment Management):** Supplier term negotiation and cash flow synchronisation aligned to project completion milestones.

| Current Ratio | 1.67x | 1.28x | Declined ↓ |

*The paradox: a company generating 160x interest coverage and SGD 17.7M operating cash flow, held at the same credit rating as the prior year. The current ratio and debt-to-equity are the two anchors — and both are addressable.*

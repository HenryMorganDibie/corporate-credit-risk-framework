# Corporate Credit Risk Analysis & Strategic Improvement Framework
**Sector focus: Construction & Engineering (Singapore)**

## 📌 Executive Overview
This repository showcases a professional framework for converting raw commercial credit bureau data into a high-level corporate financial strategy. The project centers on a "V-Shaped" recovery case study where a construction firm achieved a **144.8% revenue increase** and a **283% net income surge**, yet remained stagnant at an "Average Risk" credit rating.

The objective of this framework is to provide the analytical evidence required for credit bureau disputes and to implement internal governance controls that align a company's operational reality with its credit score.

---

## 🛠 Strategic Methodology (The "Henry C. Dibie" Framework)

I employ a three-tier approach to credit risk management:

### 1. The Paradox Analysis (Quantitative)
Traditional credit scoring models often lag behind rapid operational growth. I identify "Credit Anchors"—specific metrics (like the Current Ratio or Debt-to-Equity) that are preventing a rating upgrade despite strong profitability.
* **Metric Reconciliation:** Analyzing why the *Recommended Credit Limit* increased by 120% while the *Credit Rating* remained "Held."
* **Liquidity Stress Testing:** Evaluating the impact of rapid scaling on working capital.

### 2. Forensic Dispute Preparation (Qualitative)
Not all "Slow Payments" are created equal. I analyze payment history to differentiate between:
* **Administrative Friction:** Small balance delays (e.g., <$2k) that trigger automated score drops.
* **Industry Standards:** Identifying "Contra-settlement" models common in construction that skew average payment days (DSO).
* **Litigation Scrubbing:** Verifying the status of withdrawn or discontinued legal claims to clear "Public Record" red flags.

### 3. The 6-Month Progress Audit (Governance)
A credit score is a lagging indicator. To ensure long-term improvement, I implement an **11-point accountability framework** categorized into:
* **CP (Credit Profile):** Bureau data correction and litigation updates.
* **FM (Financial Management):** Capital structure and liability optimization.
* **PM (Payment Management):** Supplier term negotiation and cash flow synchronization.

## 📈 Key Indicators Tracked
My analysis focuses on the interplay between the following KPIs:
* **Solvency:** Current Ratio vs. Quick Ratio (Liquidity positioning).
* **Efficiency:** Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) vs. Days Payable Outstanding (DPO).
* **Profitability:** ROE and Net Profit Margin trends across 24 months.
* **Risk Profile:** Credit Bureau Score (e.g., SCCB Rating) vs. Default Probability.

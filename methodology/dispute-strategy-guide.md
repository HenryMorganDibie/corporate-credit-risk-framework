# Corporate Credit Dispute & Correction Strategy
**Protocol:** Data-Driven Rebuttal for Credit Bureau Ratings

When a company's financial performance (Revenue/Profit) outperforms its Credit Score (CA3/Average), a formal dispute or "Data Correction Package" is required. This guide details the professional preparation of that package.

## Step 1: Identification of "Ghost" Risk Factors
We scan the SCCB Comprehensive Report for items that are legally "Dead" but mathematically "Live."
* **Withdrawn Litigation:** Identifying suits marked as "Discontinued" or "Settled Out of Court" that are still weighting the "Public Record" score.
* **Outdated Paid-Up Capital:** Ensuring the bureau reflects the most recent capital injections, which directly lowers the Debt-to-Equity calculation.

## Step 2: Categorization of Payment Delinquencies
Automated scoring models treat a $500 late payment the same as a $500,000 late payment. 
* **Administrative vs. Financial:** We categorize "Slow" payments by dollar value. 
* **The Rebuttal:** We prepare a "Management Narrative" explaining that small-value delays (e.g., SGD 750) are **Administrative Friction** (invoice processing lags) rather than **Financial Incapacity**. 

## Step 3: The "Contra-Settlement" Defense
In the construction sector, companies often act as both supplier and subcontractor to the same entity.
* **The Issue:** Bureaus see a "Slow" payment.
* **The Reality:** The payment is intentionally held pending a "Contra-Charge" (offsetting what the other party owes).
* **The Strategy:** We document these industry-specific arrangements to prove the "Slow" status is a **strategic accounting choice**, not a cash flow failure.

## Step 4: Evidence Packaging (The Submission)
The final dispute package submitted to the reporting agency includes:
1.  **Notice of Discontinuance:** Certified court documents for all settled suits.
2.  **Trend Analysis Report:** A 24-month comparative table showing Revenue and Net Worth growth (The "V-Shape" Evidence).
3.  **Liquidity Narrative:** A professional letter from the CFO/Analyst explaining the temporary drop in Current Ratio as a planned expansion investment.

## Step 5: The "Rating Parity" Argument
We challenge the Bureau by pointing out internal contradictions:
> *"If the Bureau has increased the 'Recommended Credit Limit' by 120%, it acknowledges a massive increase in capacity. Therefore, maintaining an 'Average' rating is mathematically inconsistent with the provided limit. We request a manual override to a 'Strong/Good' (CA2/CA1) rating."*
